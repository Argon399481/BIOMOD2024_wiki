Project

Background

Liposomes have lipid bilayer membranes composed of phospholipids. Phospholipid molecules are amphipathic structures linked with a hydrophilic head and a hydrophobic tail. This property allows phospholipids to be self-assembled to form spherical liposomes. Modification of various biomolecules on the membrane surface and encapsulation inside the artificial cell membrane can add multiple functions to liposomes. One of the typical applications is a drug delivery systems (DDS) using liposomes as carriers. Many kinds of liposomes such as pH-sensitive or temperature-sensitive liposomes in vivo environment have been established. Ligand-modified liposomes, such as hapten liposomes that do not induce immunogenicity and stealth liposomes, are also used as a means of DDS by encapsulating drugs by emulsification methods, dry mixing methods, etc. Another promising application of liposomes is playing compartment rolls in molecular systems and robots composed of designed biomolecules. Practical examples include the control of motor protein kinesin behavior within liposomes and the amplification of single-stranded DNA inside liposomes.

Problem1

Liposomes are used not only in vitro but in vivo and in various natural environments. However, changes in the external environment, such as osmotic pressure, pH, and temperature at which the lipid bilayer undergoes a phase transition, or external forces affect the stability of liposomes. These cause concerns about leaking of the internal components and are barriers to further applications. To overcome these concerns, the research for increasing the stability of liposomes keeps going. Previous studies have attempted to stabilize liposomes by preparing a DNA cytoskeleton inside the lipid bilayer or by modifying Poly polyethylene glycols (PEG) or cholesterol on the liposome membrane surface.

Moreover, Maruyama et al. have demonstrated that modifying the liposome membrane surface with PEG significantly increases the blood circulation half-life compared to liposomes without such modifications.


Problem2

However, such tools for stabilizing liposomes might become obstacles in implementing molecular transportation systems for liposomes in the future. DNA hydrogel and PEG modification are utilized to improve the stability of liposomes. Transfering molecular signals are needed to extensive the function of artificial cells and molecular robots. In previous research, the molecular transferring via liposome membranes is realized by implementing membrane proteins such as a-hemolysin, Streptolysin O(ref), and nanopore-forming DNA nanostructures in liposome membranes. Through these nanopore devices form pores combining and transferring membranes, DNA hydrogel, and PEG probably inhibit nanodevices from forming pores in liposomes with that. As far as we know, the aforementioned nano pores target conventional Giant unilamelle vesicles (GUV), not DNA hydrogel-modification GUV. Since these nanopore devices cannot penetrate natural cell membranes, which have a complicated composition, it is similarly difficult to form pores toward GUV-coated DNA hydrogel. So, a new method of forming pore devices and systems is essential to work together to improve stability and molecular transformation systems.

Solution

In this project, we propose a nuclear envelope-like structure that is both robust and capable of future molecular transport through lipid membranes. We designed a triple-layer capsule structure, BGB (Bilayer-Gel-Bilayer), in which the DNA network is packed in two lipid bilayer layers. Referring to previous studies, liposomes were used to sandwich both sides of the DNA network so that the gel was not exposed to the inner fluid and are as well as to form a DNA network inside the liposomes to accommodate the problem2. Also, we invented the gimmick of contacting the inner bilayer and outer bilayer after DNA-origami curls attaching the inner bilayer autonomically polymerize and make the inner bilayer tubed. This gimmick allows the BGB structure to form pores and then molecular transport between the inside and outside of liposomes. BGB structure on our project will establish a new method of DDS and compartment mimicking living cell as artificial cell models.
