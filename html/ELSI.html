Future

DNA nanotechnology has provided us with a wide variety of structures, far exceeding what nature has built with DNA, ranging from arbitrary architectures made of DNA motifs, DNA origami, and DNA bricks to logic circuits, reaction networks, and self-organization on lipid surfaces, duplicating some of the functional complexity of biological systems. Identifying the factors affecting the dimerisation of DNA origami by the linker will enable the design of DNA origami with more stable dimerisation. This provides the basis for developing more stable nanostructures. The influential factor on dimerizing of triangular DNA origami that we found is useful for the realization of large scale 2D-array of DNA origami which can be widely applicable to various applications.
