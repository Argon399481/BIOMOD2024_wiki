Elucidation of the controlling factors in the formation of triangular DNA oligaminimers by linkers

Abstract

DNA origami is a method of creating nanostructures with high precision using DNA strands as building blocks. By linking multiple DNA origami to form a multimer, it is possible to create complex and functional larger-scale structures. Taking advantage of the stronger bonding strength between cytosine and guanine than between adenine and thymine, it was possible to design a triangular dimeric DNA origami with an increased number of cytosine and guanine in the linker base sequence. Here, toward the realization of large-scale 2D- arrays of DNA origami, which can be widely applicable to various applications, we investigated the dimerization of triangular DNA origami linked by linkers ssDNA that differ from the above-mentioned approach. The dimerization is strongly influenced by the deformation of a side of a triangle, which enables the dimerization rate of triangular DNA origami to increase. We demonstrate that the shape of the outermost helix has a large effect on the dimer formation rate and that introducing a spacer into the linker can significantly improve dimer formation in triangular DNA origami.
